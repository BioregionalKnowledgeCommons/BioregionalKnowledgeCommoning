# TBFF Threshold Policy Demo — 2026-03-12

## What This Proves

The TBFF write-back demo shows an end-to-end capital allocation loop:

1. A **Hub Cultivator** logs a funding decision (who got money, why, how much)
2. A **TBFF settlement** redistributes funds based on threshold-based flow funding
3. The settlement is transformed into a **citable Evidence entity** with a CAT receipt chain
4. A **claim** is created linking the evidence to the settlement assertion
5. **Threshold policy** auto-advances low-value claims (reducing governance overhead)
6. The claim is **anchored on Regen Ledger** — immutable, verifiable, auditable
7. A **proof pack** bundles everything into one archivable artifact

This means: every dollar that flows through TBFF can be traced from decision → settlement → evidence → claim → on-chain anchor. The receipt chain preserves full provenance.

## Threshold Policy (v0.1)

| Band | Amount | Behavior |
|------|--------|----------|
| **auto** | < $500 | Auto-advance to `verified` (2 system attestations) |
| **semi** | $500–$5,000 | Auto-advance to `peer_reviewed` (1 attestation; 1 more needed) |
| **manual** | > $5,000 | Stays `self_reported` (full multi-party attestation required) |

**Override:** `manual_override: true` forces manual review regardless of amount.

**Authority boundary:** Auto-advance requires two distinct reviewer identities (reviewer + operator). If only one is available, auto band advances to `peer_reviewed` only.

Band edges verified on Octo (2026-03-12):
- $499.99 → auto (verified)
- $500.00 → semi (peer_reviewed)
- $4999.99 → semi (peer_reviewed)
- $5000.00 → manual (self_reported)
