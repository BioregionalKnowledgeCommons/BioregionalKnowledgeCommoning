## 1. The Spore-admitted foundation-doctrine layer (cited upstream, read-only)

During Bundle α (Spore ADRs 0085–0090, 2026-05-23 → 05-29), Spore canon admitted the McMurtry/Sahely substrate at several layers. Two admissions are load-bearing for BKC's economic foundation, and four more compose:

### 1.1 Life-value as the 4th cross-cutting doctrine — `spore:ADR-0086`

Spore admitted `life-value-doctrine` as the **fourth cross-cutting doctrine** (`spore.canon-decision.life-value-doctrine-fourth-cross-cutting-doctrine`), sibling to `reproductive-commoning`, `boundary-commoning`, and `care-commoning`. This is a canon-object-class expansion (3 → 4 doctrines), not a glossary addition — the heaviest admission shape Spore uses. The doctrine encodes McMurtry's life-value onto-axiology (LVOA): value is the expansion of coherent life-range (thought, feeling, action); disvalue is its contraction. **Life Capital** is "the wealth of means of life and life goods that produce more without loss in cumulative yield through time" — unified by *regenerative function*, not ownership form. The **life-coherence constraint** holds that an economy degrading breathable air, potable water, public health, education, or meaningful work is not merely unjust but *malfunctioning as an economy*, because it is degrading the conditions of its own reproduction.

### 1.2 Civil Commons as a derived-glossary slug — `spore:ADR-0087`

Spore admitted `civil-commons` as a **derived-glossary slug** (`spore.canon-decision.civil-commons-derived-glossary-slug-admission`) — a vocabulary entry, NOT a doctrine. McMurtry's Civil Commons are "socially constructed systems that enable universal access to life goods and cannot function if subordinated to private profit extraction" — public water systems, sanitation, public health, education, legal/institutional frameworks, environmental protections. The inversion is the load-bearing move: orthodox economics treats these as "public expenditures" or "externalities"; McMurtry shows that markets *presuppose* the Civil Commons. They are **primary economic infrastructure — the base layer upon which all market activity depends**, and their erosion is a direct contraction of Life Capital.

### 1.3 Care as a scope-condition on the care doctrine — `spore:ADR-0088`

Spore scope-conditioned the care-cluster onto its existing care framing (`spore.canon-decision.care-cluster-scope-condition-adr-0045`; this is the Bundle-α scope-condition, not the pre-Bundle-α ADR-0045 parent). The Maturana lineage — love as the biological grounding of social coexistence, structural-coupling as the relational mechanism — is the substrate. For BKC this matters because commitment pooling is, at root, a **care-and-trust** infrastructure: a pledge has value "because the community trusts the pledger" (`bkc.commitment-pooling` §2.1), and pool nourishment is governed by relational stewardship, not formulas.

### 1.4 Three composing scope-conditions — `spore:ADR-0085`, `spore:ADR-0089`, `spore:ADR-0090`

- **`spore:ADR-0085`** admitted trap-shape vocabulary including `golden-calf-trap` and the `recursive-audit-method` (`spore.canon-decision.trap-shape-vocab-and-recursive-audit-method`). `golden-calf-trap` is the SHAPE-OF (not equivalent-to) the older `substitution-trap`: a proxy elevated to the thing it was meant to track. McMurtry's GDP Paradox — a metric that rises while the system it measures deteriorates — is the paradigm-case at economic-civilization scale.
- **`spore:ADR-0089`** scope-conditioned `margin-as-reserve` onto the maintenance-economics foundation doc (`spore.canon-decision.margin-as-reserve-scope-condition-f9`): resilience requires holding reserve / slack, not optimizing it away.
- **`spore:ADR-0090`** scope-conditioned `perception-as-power` onto representation-authority (`spore.canon-decision.perception-as-power-scope-condition-f4`): the power to define what counts as success (e.g. GDP, money-value) is itself a form of power, and mis-defining it is the "proxy error."

These six are read-only references to Spore canon. BKC inherits the vocabulary; it does not author or alter it.

## 2. McMurtry's economic foundation, in McMurtry's terms (via Sahely 2026-01-10)

Per the Spore-side `spore.connection.sahely-money-growth-to-life-coherence` substrate-grammar map, Sahely's 2026-01-10 paper carries verbatim McMurtry citations with full McMurtry-primary attribution. The three structural moves that ground BKC's economic foundation:

1. **Civil Commons as primary economic infrastructure** (McMurtry, via Sahely §9): "the base layer upon which all market activity depends." Markets and private enterprise cannot exist "without stable ecosystems, educated populations, healthy workers, enforceable contracts, and social trust." Erosion of the Civil Commons is "a direct contraction of life capital."

2. **Life Capital as regenerative-function-not-ownership-form** (McMurtry, via Sahely §8): "The wealth of means of life and life goods that produce more without loss in cumulative yield through time." The temporal-regeneration criterion — *yield through time without depletion* — is what unifies heterogeneous institutional forms (ecological systems, public health, education, social trust, knowledge systems). "What unites these is not ownership form but regenerative function."

3. **The selection-mechanism diagnostic** (McMurtry, via Sahely §11): defining capital monetarily, efficiency as profit, development as transaction volume "selects for activities that convert life capital into money claims." This is "not a moral accusation; it is a selection mechanism." Firms that extract fastest outperform those that regenerate — "until the underlying systems fail." What looks like market success "can be systemic self-consumption."

McMurtry-primary attribution is load-bearing: McMurtry is the framework-originator (across *Unequal Freedoms* 1998, *Value Wars* 2002, *Philosophy and World Problems* UNESCO 2009, *The Cancer Stage of Capitalism* 2nd ed. 2013); Sahely is the application-layer adapter extending LVOA to economics. McMurtry-primary corpus was not directly consulted for this note; citations carry through Sahely's faithful verbatim reading per the Spore-side bridge note's source discipline.

## 3. How BKC's production layer enacts the economic foundation

This is the core of the bridge: McMurtry articulates the requirement; BKC's commitment-pooling and commitment-economy production layer operationalizes it. The mapping is not metaphorical — it is point-for-point against the running system.

### 3.1 Civil Commons as the boundary-set within which life-value is collectively secured

McMurtry's Civil Commons names *what* must be stewarded: socially constructed systems enabling universal access to life goods. BKC's commitment pool, with its **curation–valuation–limitation–exchange (CVLE)** decomposition, is the *operational mechanism* a community uses to steward exactly such systems. The four functions are the missing operational decomposition for what McMurtry names as foundation-doctrine:

| McMurtry requirement | BKC pool function (`bkc.commitment-pooling`) | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| Who counts as an eligible recipient / what counts as a life-good | **Registry (Curation)** — three orthogonal operations: create / pledge / verify (§7) | Stewards curate which commitments belong in which pool ("this commitment fits our pool's purpose and bioregion") |
| Valuing life-goods in life-coherent, not money-proxy, terms | **Value Index (Valuation)** — routing scorer + steward-set limits | Community signals what's valued; "value arises from trusted capacity to deliver, not market price" (§2.1) |
| Preventing extractive over-issuance that erodes the regenerative substrate | **Limiter (Limitation)** — TBFF threshold bands + per-pool caps (§5.1) | "Everyone has a cap on how much they can take on"; pool safety limits protect the commons |
| Coordinating exchange across pools / bioregions consistent with life-capital regeneration | **Fee Policy + Vault-Settlement (Exchange)** — multi-hop routing, SwapPool, cycle clearing | Moving value where it's needed and home again, "so safety is local yet preserved hop-by-hop" (§5) |

The coherence is operational at the governance layer in particular. McMurtry's "Civil Commons cannot function if subordinated to private profit extraction" maps onto BKC's insistence that pool governance is *constitutive of*, not adjacent to, the economy's life-coherent operation: stewards (not markets) decide whose needs are prioritized (`bkc.commitment-economy-vision` §2.4, §6 "Caring"), and forkability (§7.5 / §9.7) is the antidote to possessive stewardship — "you cannot capture a pool because the underlying commitments are not yours to hold."

In short: **Civil Commons is the boundary-set within which life-value is collectively secured; the commitment pool is how a bioregion draws and maintains that boundary in practice.** The pool's curation says what is inside the commons; its limiter prevents extractive over-draw; its governance keeps the boundary answerable to life rather than to profit.

### 3.2 Life Capital ↔ the four metabolic functions

McMurtry's Life Capital — regenerative-function-not-ownership-form, yield-through-time-without-depletion — is exactly the value-form BKC's commitment economy is built to circulate. `bkc.commitment-economy-vision` names four metabolic functions (provisioning, redistribution, activation, repair) that together make a complete commitment economy. Each enacts a facet of Life Capital stewardship:

| Life Capital facet (McMurtry/Sahely) | BKC metabolic function (`bkc.commitment-economy-vision` §2) |
|---|---|
| Regenerative capacity made legible and routable, not monetary assets | **Provisioning** — commitment pooling: capacity (labor, goods, stewardship, knowledge) made legible "as a pooled commons"; value from "trusted redeemability, not external price signals" |
| Sufficiency floor — basic life-needs met before abundance is pursued | **Redistribution** — threshold-based flow funding (TBFF): overflow above comfort routes to those below threshold; "participation in this economy does not cost you your wellbeing" (§4) |
| Expanding *secure access* to life goods across the population and through time | **Activation** — community practices (mapping workshops, Mweria/Meitheal labor circles) that make commitments legible; "the commitment economy starts when people formalize what they can offer and need — not when capital arrives" (§2.3) |
| Recovery from harm; protecting the conditions of reproduction | **Repair / immune system** — limits, loss waterfall, disputes, forkability, consent boundaries, bounded autonomy (§8): "what makes the economy safe enough for people to participate without risking their wellbeing" |

The "regenerative-function-not-ownership-form" criterion is precisely BKC's distinction between commitment value and ReFi speculation (`bkc.commitment-pooling` §6): ReFi tokenizes *future value* (speculative); commitment pooling makes *prior obligations* — capacity, labor, stewardship already being performed — legible as economic signals. Sarafu's 84% positive income impact and 1,200+ acres restored are Life Capital regenerating, measured by yield-through-time, not by claim-value at a core.

### 3.3 The selection-mechanism diagnostic ↔ the netting yield and dependency-quality

McMurtry's selection-mechanism — money-defined capital selects for converting life capital into money claims — is the failure mode BKC's design is built to *avoid by construction*. Where orthodox economics rewards extraction-velocity until the substrate fails, BKC's commitment economy measures progress as **dependency quality** (`bkc.commitment-economy-vision` §9.9; `bkc.commitment-economy-design`): reducing extractive dependency (donor-cycle, chokepoint, learning-loop, metabolic) while strengthening *healthy interdependence* (reciprocal, visible, chosen, distributed). The CLC **netting yield** — `(gross routed value − net external liquidity injected) / gross routed value` — is the proxy for reduced extractive metabolic dependency: higher netting = more internal life-capital circulation, less life-substance drained out through money-sequence channels.

This is the same shape Spore admitted as `golden-calf-trap` (`spore:ADR-0085`): a proxy (money, GDP, token price) elevated above the reality it stood for. BKC's anti-fragility rule is the direct counter (`bkc.commitment-economy-vision` §8, Ruddick): "Fee credits, routing preference, and any network rewards must be gated by measurable redemption and fulfillment signals, not token price and not narrative momentum. If financial upside can grow while redemption quality decays, the network will drift toward speculation and enclosure." Gating rewards on *fulfillment* rather than *price* is the `recursive-audit-method` applied to a credit network — turning the measuring grammar back on itself so the proxy cannot ossify.

## 5. Summary

McMurtry's life-value onto-axiology gives an economy its answerability to life: Civil Commons as primary economic infrastructure, Life Capital as regenerative-function-not-ownership-form, the life-coherence constraint as the viability criterion an economy must not violate. Spore admitted this substrate during Bundle α — `life-value-doctrine` as the 4th cross-cutting doctrine (`spore:ADR-0086`) and `civil-commons` as a derived-glossary slug (`spore:ADR-0087`), with care, trap-shape, margin, and perception scope-conditions composing (`spore:ADR-0088` / `0085` / `0089` / `0090`).

BKC's commitment-pooling and commitment-economy production layer **already enacts this foundation**: the CVLE pool is the mechanism that draws and maintains the Civil Commons boundary-set within which life-value is collectively secured; the four metabolic functions steward Life Capital's yield-through-time; and the dependency-quality / netting-yield / fulfillment-gated-rewards design is BKC's structural defense against the money-as-false-proxy selection mechanism McMurtry diagnoses. Civil Commons is the *what*; the commitment pool is the *how a bioregion does it*.
