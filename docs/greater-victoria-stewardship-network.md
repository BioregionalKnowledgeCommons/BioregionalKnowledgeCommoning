# Greater Victoria Stewardship Network

The Greater Victoria stewardship network is a web of organizations collaborating on ecological restoration and bioregional governance in the southern Vancouver Island region of the Salish Sea bioregion. The Victoria Landscape Hub serves as the coordination node, connecting restoration practitioners, flow funding infrastructure, and knowledge commoning tools.

## Victoria Landscape Hub

The Victoria Landscape Hub is the coordination body for landscape-level restoration in Greater Victoria. It operates as part of Regenerate Cascadia's Hub Cultivator program, stewarding flow funding decisions and connecting member organizations to shared resources.

Key activities:
- **Hub Cultivator program**: A trusted steward (currently Darren and Polly in Victoria) receives a capital allocation from Regenerate Cascadia and makes direct funding decisions to ground-level projects. The first allocation directed $250 CAD to Mycopunks for a mycelium remediation pilot.
- **Volunteer coordination**: Sarah from VLH coordinates volunteer recruitment (40 hours/month) and provides workshop space access (twice per week, approximately $800/month equivalent).
- **Commitment pool**: The Victoria Landscape Hub Restoration Pool aggregates verified commitments from member organizations. The pool transitioned from forming to active status when pledged capacity exceeded its activation threshold.

Member organizations include Kinship Earth, Mycopunks, and Regenerate Cascadia's local presence.

## Kinship Earth

Kinship Earth is a bioregional stewardship organization that operates the Earth Regeneration Fund. It launched flow funding across six pilot bioregions in September 2024: Barichara, Cascadia, Forests of the Northeast, Greater Tkaronto Bioregion, Northern Andes, and Ogallala. Cascadia's implementation flows through Regenerate Cascadia, making Kinship Earth a key upstream funder for the Greater Victoria network.

Restoration contributions from Kinship Earth in Greater Victoria:
- **Soil monitoring**: Randy from Kinship Earth provides soil monitoring equipment loans (4 kits) and ecological assessment expertise (80 hours) to local restoration projects.
- **Ecological assessment**: Professional ecological surveys that inform watershed restoration planning.
- **Narrative accountability**: Kinship Earth governs reporting through four reflective questions rather than quantitative metrics: *What inspired you? What surprised you? What challenged you? What moved you?* This trust-based model prioritizes narrative over compliance.

Kinship Earth's flow funding model is trust-based and low-paperwork: a funder provides capital to landscape-connected stewards who distribute smaller grants based on direct knowledge of local needs.

## Mycopunks

Mycopunks is a mycological and ecological restoration collective based in Victoria. Five members (including Darren) experiment with Threshold-Based Flow Funding (TBFF) — an algorithmic redistribution protocol where each participant sets a maximum threshold, and overflow above that threshold redistributes according to weighted allocation preferences.

Mycopunks' contributions to the stewardship network:
- **Mycelium remediation**: Pilot projects applying fungal remediation techniques to contaminated or degraded sites in Greater Victoria.
- **TBFF protocol development**: Running a live experiment with $32K across 5 participants, $8K maximum thresholds each. The protocol is deployed on Base Sepolia testnet targeting Celo mainnet.
- **Knowledge sharing**: Connecting mycological science with bioregional governance through the Victoria Landscape Hub.

Mycopunks receives flow funding through the Hub Cultivator program and participates in the Victoria Landscape Hub Restoration Pool.

## Regenerate Cascadia

Regenerate Cascadia is the parent program for bioregional organizing across the Cascadia bioregion, founded by Brandon Letsinger and Clare Attwell. Its BioFi infrastructure maps landscape-level regeneration teams to ecoregional coordination to bioregional aggregation — "like water through a watershed."

The Landscape Hub Cultivator program supports up to 10 landscape groups across Cascadia, with 13 landscape groups, approximately 40 stewards, and $80K total support in Phase 2 (March–June 2026). The Victoria Landscape Hub is one of these landscape groups.

Regenerate Cascadia provides:
- **Capital allocation**: Flow funding from Kinship Earth and other sources distributed through Hub Cultivator stewards.
- **Governance infrastructure**: Bioregional mapping workshops that surface offers, needs, watershed indicators, and project portfolios from landscape groups.
- **Network coordination**: Connecting landscape groups across three eco-regions (Salish Sea, Columbia River, Willamette Valley) with approximately 35 stewards.

## Network Relationships

The organizations relate through overlapping governance, funding, and restoration activities:

- **Kinship Earth** funds **Regenerate Cascadia** through flow funding, which distributes capital to **Victoria Landscape Hub** via the Hub Cultivator program.
- **Victoria Landscape Hub** coordinates **Mycopunks**, **Kinship Earth** (local presence), and other restoration practitioners in Greater Victoria.
- **Mycopunks** receives funding through VLH and experiments with TBFF protocol that complements the Hub Cultivator model.
- **Regenerate Cascadia** provides the parent organizational umbrella, connecting Victoria's network to the broader Cascadia bioregion including the Cascadia Bioregion Stewardship Pool.

## Restoration Practices

The network's restoration focus areas in Greater Victoria include:
- **Watershed restoration**: Coordinated efforts across local waterways and riparian zones.
- **Riparian planting**: Vegetation restoration along stream banks and shorelines, led by Regenerate Cascadia landscape groups.
- **Soil monitoring**: Equipment-based soil health tracking using Kinship Earth's monitoring kits.
- **Ecological assessment**: Professional surveys informing restoration priorities.
- **Mycological remediation**: Fungal-based soil and habitat remediation through Mycopunks' pilot projects.
- **Bioregional mapping**: Community workshops documenting local ecological knowledge, restoration needs, and organizational capacity.
