### Track-Specific Blurbs

**Open Track / "Agents that Cooperate":**
> Octo is an AI agent that helps bioregional communities cooperate through structured commitments. It extracts offers, wants, and limits from natural language, routes them to stewarded pools using a deterministic 5-factor scorer, and proves fulfillment through dual-chain attestation (Regen Ledger + Celo EAS). Four federated nodes across the Salish Sea and Front Range share entities through a consent-aware membrane ‚Äî trust through kept promises, not token mechanics.

**Protocol Labs ‚Äî "Agents With Receipts" (ERC-8004):**
> Octo is registered as agentId 1855 on 8004scan (Celo mainnet). On-chain ERC-8004 metadata updated via `setAgentURI` ([TX `0x2b8f35...`](https://celoscan.io/tx/0x2b8f35697f233eb9ff1e3d3cf84347baeff4a5498102ac877f9320cbedd6379c)) ‚Äî `registrations` now populated with 3 deployed contracts: VCV Token (`0x4CDb98Ff...`), TBFFSettler (`0x2a13c4eB...`), SwapPool (`0x181E36AD...`). The A2A endpoint at `/.well-known/agent.json` is healthy (HTTP 200, TLSv1.3, 15 tools exposed); any 8004scan 404 is a stale crawler artifact. Every pipeline step produces verifiable on-chain receipts: VCV mint TXs, TBFF settlement TXs, EAS attestation UIDs, content-hashed proof packs, CAT receipt chains. `agent_log.json` is a Protocol Labs submission artifact keyed to ERC-8004 identity capturing the full execution trace.

**Protocol Labs ‚Äî "Let the Agent Cook":**
> Octo runs a complete autonomous decision loop: (1) Discover ‚Äî transcribe audio via Whisper, (2) Plan ‚Äî extract commitment candidates via GPT-4o-mini, (3) Execute ‚Äî create commitments, route to pools, mint VCV tokens on Celo, (4) Verify ‚Äî TBFF settlement with multi-participant redistribution, (5) Submit ‚Äî anchor proof to Regen Ledger + attest on Celo EAS. The full loop runs unattended via `demo-full-loop.sh`.

**Octant ‚Äî "Public Goods":**
> BKC is a public goods data infrastructure: 2,759 entities across 4 federated nodes collecting bioregional knowledge (organizations, projects, locations, practices). The TBFF mechanism evaluates public goods commitments through threshold-based pooling: communities pledge restoration/stewardship commitments, the routing scorer evaluates fit, and settlement redistributes capacity based on needs-weighted thresholds. All data is consent-gated and governed by stewards.

**Filecoin ‚Äî "Agentic Storage":**
> Proof packs (claim + evidence + attestations + on-chain anchors) are content-addressed JSON artifacts archived on IPFS/Filecoin via Storacha. The agent autonomously generates and stores verifiable records of bioregional commitments ‚Äî permanent, tamper-evident, and retrievable by CID. Deployed on Celo mainnet with Filecoin backing for archival.
