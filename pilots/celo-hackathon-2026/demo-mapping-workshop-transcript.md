# Bioregional Mapping Workshop — Victoria Landscape Hub
## Date: March 2026
## Participants: Sarah (Regenerate Cascadia), Randy (Kinship Earth), Alex (Mycopunks), Jordan (Victoria Landscape Hub)
## Facilitator: Jordan
## Bioregion: Salish Sea

---

**Jordan:** Welcome everyone. This is our quarterly mapping workshop for the Victoria Landscape Hub. The purpose today is to map what each of our organizations can contribute to the shared restoration work happening across the Salish Sea bioregion. Let's go around and talk about what you're able to offer for the coming season.

**Sarah:** Thanks Jordan. At Regenerate Cascadia we've been focused on watershed restoration, especially riparian planting and stream bank stabilization. For this coming season, I can commit our field crew for about 200 hours of restoration labor. That covers the period from April through September. We've got certified arborists and experienced planters. What we'd want in return is access to monitoring data from partner sites — we need that for our grant reporting to show cross-site impact.

**Randy:** That's great Sarah. We can definitely help with monitoring. Kinship Earth has been building out our soil monitoring program. We have four portable soil monitoring kits — each one has pH meters, moisture sensors, and sampling tools. I'm committing to lending those out on a quarterly basis. So that's 4 kits per quarter available to anyone in the network. The only condition is they need to come back in working condition, and we'd love to get the soil data reports back so we can build our regional dataset.

**Jordan:** Randy that's actually a practice we should document — the shared equipment lending with data reciprocity. We've seen similar patterns in the tool libraries across the region.

**Randy:** Right, exactly. It's become a real pattern for us. The reciprocity isn't just about getting the kits back — it's about building a shared dataset that everyone benefits from.

**Alex:** From Mycopunks, we're offering something a bit different. We've been developing mycoremediation techniques for contaminated soils. I can commit to running a pilot at two sites in the watershed — that's about 40 hours of specialized labor over the next two months. We'll do site assessment, inoculation, and follow-up monitoring. What we want is sites with existing soil data so we can measure the before and after. So Randy, your monitoring kits would be perfect for that.

**Randy:** Absolutely, we can coordinate on that. We'd claim that mycoremediation has shown 30-40% reduction in heavy metal contamination in our previous trials. I've got data from three sites to back that up.

**Jordan:** And from the Victoria Landscape Hub side, what I can offer is volunteer coordination. I'll commit 8 hours per week of my time to coordinating volunteers across all of our partner projects. That includes scheduling, training logistics, and making sure volunteers get matched to the right sites. I see this as an ongoing commitment — at least through the end of the year.

**Sarah:** That's incredibly valuable. One thing I notice is we're building a real practice around coordinated multi-organization restoration. Each of us brings a different piece — labor, equipment, specialized knowledge, coordination. The pattern is that no single organization can do this alone, but when we pool our commitments, we can cover entire watersheds.

**Jordan:** I agree. I'd go further and say this is a pattern of bioregional commitment pooling — organizations making specific, time-bound offers that get aggregated into a portfolio that's more than the sum of its parts.

**Alex:** And there's a protocol emerging too. The way we're doing it — mapping commitments, matching them to needs, tracking through seasons — that could be documented as a replicable coordination protocol for other bioregions.

**Jordan:** Great observation. Let me also note the evidence we're building: Randy mentioned the mycoremediation contamination reduction data, and Sarah has the cross-site monitoring data. These are concrete claims we can verify and build on.

**Sarah:** One more thing — my commitment of 200 hours has a limit. We can't work on sites more than 2 hours drive from Victoria. And we need at least 2 weeks notice for scheduling.

**Jordan:** Understood. Let's make sure those constraints are captured. Alright, I think we have a strong set of commitments for this quarter. Let me summarize what we've mapped today before we close.
